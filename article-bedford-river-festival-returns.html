Bedford River Festival Returns After Three-Year Break With Expanded Programme

By Peter Hartwell • 13 June 2026 • 3 min read
Awaiting verification: The Bedfordshire Free Press has not yet independently confirmed the names, figures and quotations in this report against primary sources. Details will be confirmed, corrected or updated as official records become available.

The Embankment along the Great Ouse will host the two-day festival on 19–20 July 2026. Image: illustrative.

Dragon boat racing, live music, street food and family activities return to Bedford’s famous Embankment

Bedford's iconic River Festival is set to return this summer after a three-year hiatus, with organisers promising the largest and most diverse programme in the event's history. Tens of thousands of visitors are expected to descend on the town's famous Embankment for a two-day celebration of riverside life, culture, and community.

The festival, which was last held in 2023 before being postponed due to funding pressures and organisational changes, will take place on the weekend of 19–20 July 2026. Entry will be free, with donations welcomed to support the town's volunteer community groups.

What’s on

The 2026 programme includes dragon boat racing on the Great Ouse, with more than thirty teams registered from across Bedfordshire and beyond. The river course will feature heats and finals throughout both days, with a trophy presentation ceremony on the Sunday afternoon.

A mainstage music programme on the Embankment will run from midday to 9pm on Saturday and from noon to 7pm on Sunday, featuring local acts, a community choir showcase, and headline performances from two regional bands yet to be formally announced. The event's famous parade of decorated river craft will take place on Saturday evening.

Family activities include a water-sports taster zone — offering kayaking, paddleboarding, and open canoeing for children and adults — as well as craft stalls, street food from more than forty vendors, and a dedicated children's performance area with circus skills workshops and storytelling sessions.

Three years in the making

The River Festival has been a fixture of Bedford's summer calendar since the 1980s, drawing visitors from across Bedfordshire and neighbouring counties. The 2020 edition was cancelled due to the pandemic, the 2021 event reduced to a single day, and organisers cited ongoing financial constraints and volunteer fatigue in postponing the 2024 and 2025 editions.

The 2026 return has been made possible through a combination of renewed investment from Bedford Borough Council, sponsorship from local businesses, and a successful Arts Council England grant application. The event is now managed by a newly constituted community interest company drawing on the expertise of former organisers alongside fresh volunteers.

"The River Festival belongs to Bedford," said the festival's newly appointed chair. "There's been a real sense of loss without it, and an enormous amount of goodwill to bring it back. We've spent two years planning this properly, and we're confident this will be the best edition yet."

Economic boost expected

Bedford Borough Council's economic development team estimates the two-day festival will generate approximately £1.2 million in direct visitor spending for the town, with accommodation, restaurants, and retail benefiting alongside the festival's own vendors and traders.

Hotel and B&B operators in central Bedford have reported strong advance bookings, with several properties already at or near capacity for the festival weekend. Day visitors are expected to arrive by rail, with Thameslink services providing regular connections from London and the wider Midland Main Line corridor.

Getting there

The Embankment is accessible on foot from Bedford town centre (approximately 10 minutes from Bedford station). Parking will be available at designated sites on the outskirts of town, with shuttle buses running throughout both days. Full programme details and volunteering opportunities are expected to be published on the festival's website shortly.
