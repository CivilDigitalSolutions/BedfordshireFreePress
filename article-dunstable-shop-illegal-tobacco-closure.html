Dunstable Shop Shut Down After Repeated Illegal Tobacco and Vape Sales

By Peter Hartwell • 14 June 2026 • 3 min read

Closure orders make it a criminal offence to enter a premises during the specified period. Image: illustrative.

Luton Magistrates’ Court grants three-month closure order against Dunstable High Street South shop following resident complaints and Trading Standards investigation.

A Dunstable shop has been ordered to close for three months after Central Bedfordshire Council’s Trading Standards team secured a court order following repeated concerns about the sale of illegal tobacco and vaping products.

Luton Magistrates’ Court granted the closure order against Mega Romanska on High Street South in Dunstable, after an investigation triggered by residents raising ongoing concerns with the council. The order makes it a criminal offence for anyone to enter the premises during the three-month closure period.

Background to the Investigation

Central Bedfordshire Council said its Trading Standards officers were alerted to the shop following a pattern of complaints from local residents about suspected illegal sales. Trading Standards investigators subsequently visited the premises and found evidence of illegal tobacco and vaping products being sold.

The closure order is one of the most significant enforcement tools available to local authorities in tackling the illegal tobacco trade, effectively shutting down the business for a fixed period rather than simply issuing a fine. Breaching a closure order is itself a criminal offence.

“We take the sale of illegal tobacco and vaping products extremely seriously,” a Central Bedfordshire Council spokesperson said. “These products are unregulated, untaxed and potentially much more harmful than legal alternatives. They also undercut legitimate retailers who follow the rules.”

A Wider Enforcement Picture

The Dunstable case is the latest in a sustained enforcement effort by Central Bedfordshire’s Trading Standards team against illegal tobacco and vape retailers. In the past year, the council has issued more than £5,000 in fines to businesses across the area found to be selling illegal vaping products, in addition to securing several closure orders against repeat offenders.

Earlier closure orders in Dunstable’s High Street South area targeted two other shops — Dunya Mini Market and Queensway News — both of which had prior prosecution histories before being ordered to shut. In the case of Dunya Mini Market, investigators discovered that both the shop and the flat above had been modified to conceal goods from inspectors.

Why Illegal Tobacco Matters

Illegal tobacco and counterfeit vaping products present several public health risks beyond those associated with legal alternatives. Because they are unregulated, their contents are unknown and may include higher-than-declared levels of nicotine or harmful additives. Counterfeit vapes in particular have been associated with fire risks due to substandard batteries.

Trading Standards officers across Bedfordshire continue to operate in partnership with Bedfordshire Police and the UK Border Force to intercept illegal tobacco products before they reach the retail stage.

How to Report Concerns

Residents who suspect a local business of selling illegal tobacco or vaping products can report their concerns to Central Bedfordshire Council’s Trading Standards team at centralbedfordshire.gov.uk, or anonymously via the Citizens Advice consumer helpline. The council has indicated that tip-offs from residents have been a key factor in recent enforcement action.
