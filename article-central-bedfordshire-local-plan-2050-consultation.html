Residents Shape Bedfordshire’s Future as Local Plan 2050 Scoping Consultation Closes

By Peter Hartwell • 14 June 2026 • 4 min read

The Local Plan 2050 will shape development across Central Bedfordshire for the next 24 years. Image: illustrative.

The first formal step in crafting Central Bedfordshire’s planning framework to 2050 is complete — now comes the analysis.

Central Bedfordshire Council has completed the first formal step in crafting the planning document that will govern development across the region for the next quarter of a century, after its Local Plan 2050 scoping consultation closed on 3 June 2026.

The consultation, which was open to all residents, businesses and organisations with an interest in the area’s future, asked participants to identify the key issues that the new Local Plan should address — from housing supply and transport links to environmental protection and the impact of climate change.

What Is the Local Plan 2050?

The Local Plan 2050 will be the legal framework that determines what gets built where across Central Bedfordshire until 2050. Once adopted, it will guide decisions on planning applications for homes, employment sites, retail developments, green spaces and infrastructure investment across the entire council area.

The scoping consultation was required under new legislation introduced by the government in March 2026, which mandated that local authorities undertake a formal public scoping exercise before writing any new local plan — a step designed to ensure communities are consulted on priorities before specific sites or policies are identified.

“At this stage, we are not making decisions about specific development sites,” the council confirmed in its guidance materials. “We are asking for views on the key issues facing the area.”

What Were Residents Asked?

The consultation covered housing (type, scale and location of future homes, including affordable provision), transport and connectivity (road network pressures, public transport gaps and active travel infrastructure), environment and biodiversity (green belt, nature recovery, and protection of valued landscapes including the Chiltern Hills), climate change (how development can support net zero and build flood resilience), and economy and employment (balance between residential and commercial growth, support for local businesses and town centres).

What Happens Next?

Council officers will now analyse all responses received before the 3 June deadline. The feedback will be used to inform the scope of the Local Plan — in other words, to determine which topics and issues the plan will need to address in detail, before any policies are actually drafted.

The next stage — known as the Regulation 18 “Issues and Options” consultation — is expected in due course, at which point more detailed questions, including possible approaches to housing numbers and site identification, will be put to residents.

Planning consultancy J&J Planning & Design noted in its April 2026 update that the timetable for Central Bedfordshire’s Local Plan remains subject to the evolving national planning policy landscape, with government reforms continuing to shape the plan-making process across England.

Why It Matters

Central Bedfordshire is one of England’s fastest-growing areas, with significant development pressure from its position between London, Milton Keynes and Luton. The Local Plan 2050 will need to balance continued housing demand against protecting the countryside, managing infrastructure — particularly roads and schools — and supporting the council’s climate commitments.

Residents who did not participate in the scoping consultation will have further opportunities to have their say as the Local Plan progresses. The council encourages anyone with a long-term interest in the area’s development to register for updates at localplan@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk.

Sources: Central Bedfordshire Council; J&J Planning & Design.
