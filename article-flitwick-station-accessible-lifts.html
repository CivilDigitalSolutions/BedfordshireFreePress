Flitwick Station to Receive Accessible Lifts Under Govia Thameslink Upgrade

By Peter Hartwell • 13 June 2026 • 3 min read
Awaiting verification: The Bedfordshire Free Press has not yet independently confirmed the names, figures and quotations in this report against primary sources. Details will be confirmed, corrected or updated as official records become available.

Flitwick station on the Midland Main Line. Image: illustrative.

Step-free access coming to Mid Bedfordshire's busiest Thameslink stop

Flitwick railway station is set to become fully step-free after Govia Thameslink Railway (GTR) confirmed it will install accessible platform lifts as part of the government's Access for All programme. The upgrade, expected to begin later this year, has been welcomed by disability groups and local councillors who have long campaigned for improved facilities at the Mid Bedfordshire stop.

The station, which sits on the Thameslink Midland Main Line and handles tens of thousands of journeys annually between Bedford and London St Pancras, currently requires passengers to use a steep flight of steps to reach its two platforms. The new lifts will provide level, unassisted access for wheelchair users, passengers with pushchairs, and anyone with mobility difficulties.

What the upgrade includes

Works are expected to encompass the installation of two platform lifts connecting the footbridge to each platform, new tactile paving and audible announcements, upgraded passenger information screens, and improved lighting throughout the station underpass. A temporary footbridge may be in place during construction to maintain access.

Network Rail, which owns and manages the station infrastructure, will carry out the civil engineering works in partnership with GTR. A formal timeline has not yet been published, but sources familiar with the programme suggest construction is scheduled to begin in autumn 2026, with completion targeted for spring 2027.

Long campaign comes good

Campaigners and local residents have pressed for step-free access at Flitwick for more than a decade. The station serves a catchment area spanning several Mid Bedfordshire villages and is a key interchange for commuters heading into London. Its inaccessibility has been a source of frustration for disabled passengers, who have been required to use alternative stations or arrange specialist assistance.

"This is genuinely transformative news for our community," said one local resident who uses a wheelchair and has previously had to travel to Bedford or Luton Airport Parkway — both further from home — to board a Thameslink service. "It shouldn't have taken this long, but we're relieved it's finally happening."

Access for All programme

The Access for All programme has funded step-free access improvements at more than 200 stations across England, Scotland and Wales since its launch in 2006. Funding for the latest tranche was announced as part of the government's rail reform agenda, with a focus on smaller suburban and rural stations that had been passed over in earlier rounds.

GTR's regional director noted that Flitwick was among a batch of stations identified as having high footfall relative to the complexity and cost of making them step-free, making it a strong candidate for the programme. Exact costings for the Flitwick works have not been disclosed.

What passengers can do now

Passengers requiring step-free assistance at Flitwick are currently advised to contact National Rail Passenger Assist, which can arrange staff support and ramp deployment. Assistance should be booked at least two hours before travel. Details are available at nationalrail.co.uk or by calling 0800 022 3720.

The announcement comes as GTR prepares for wider timetable changes on the Midland Main Line, following continued upgrades to track and signalling between Bedford and London.
